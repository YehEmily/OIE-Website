Olin Introductory Experience

Olin 101. This is a class that will introduce you to Olin and its unique culture, teach you how to make the most of your college experience, and help you understand everything you have to be excited about while you're here.

Course Policies

Expectations and Grading

  1. Classes start at 9:00 AM on Wednesdays. Grading is effort and participation based, so go to class and do your best!
  2. You may miss up to 2 classes and 2 activites or assignments without consequences. Contact Adva or a NINJA before class if you won't be able to attend a class or activity or complete an assignment on time. If you need to miss more classes, activities, or assignments, contact Adva or a NINJA as soon as you can.
  3. You are expected to spend at least 3 hours on this class per week. Some classes will be 3 hours long, after which you will not be expected to do further work or attend affiliated activities. Other classes will be about 1.5 hours long, after which you will be expected to complete GCSP assignments and/or attend affiliated activities.

Classes, Affiliated Activities, and GCSP Assignments

  • Regular Classes (~1.5 hours): engage with a variety of students and faculty on a different topic every week; mostly interactive activity- or discussion-based classes that may meet as a large group and/or split up into smaller groups
  • Extended Classes (~3 hours): cover topics that require extra time; replaces additional work requirements for the week and is not optional (unlike Affiliated Activities and GCSP Assignments)
  • Affiliated Activities (~1.5 hours): complement in-class topic of the week; you may accumulate attendance at Affiliated Activities by attending more activities during one week to skip one the next, and if an Affiliated Activity does not last 1.5 hours, it will still count towards completing the work requirement for the week
  • GCSP Assignment (~1.5 hours): for students who process information internally or prefer to create visual or written representations of their experiences; can utilize in-class and Affiliated Activity material for documentation, as well as any other relevant experiences

Please note that you are required to attend the TIPS and Title IX and Bystander Intervention activities.
